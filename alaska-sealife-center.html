The Problem

Alaska's ocean wildlife has no one else to call.

When a sea otter pup washes ashore orphaned, when a stranded seal needs emergency care, when harbor seal populations start declining and no one knows why, the Alaska SeaLife Center is the only institution authorized to respond. That is not a small thing. It is an enormous responsibility, and it depends entirely on people like you.

Climate change is reshaping Alaska's ocean.

Marine heatwaves, shifting prey populations, and warming waters are driving more wildlife strandings and making scientific monitoring more urgent than ever.

Seasonal funding leaves gaps in critical programs.

Visitor revenue spikes in summer and dips in winter. But the animals that need care and the research that must continue do not take a seasonal break.

Caring people feel helpless from a distance.

You love Alaska's wildlife. You follow the rescue stories. But without a simple, direct way to plug in, that feeling of wanting to help stays just out of reach.

Why It Works

Your $25 a month does something most donations never do.

Most donations come in once and disappear. Monthly gifts create a current, a steady, dependable force that funds rescue operations in January, research projects in March, and education programs all summer long. You become the infrastructure that makes everything else possible.

Questions? We have answers.

Where does my monthly gift actually go?
Your gift supports the Alaska SeaLife Center's three core programs: wildlife rescue and rehabilitation, ongoing marine science research, and hands-on education for students and communities across Alaska. In 2023, the Center spent over $2.7 million directly on research, education, and wildlife response programs combined.
Can I cancel my monthly gift at any time?
Yes, absolutely. You can cancel or change your recurring donation at any time by calling Development Associate Laura Swihart at 907-224-6337 or emailing development@alaskasealife.org. There are no penalties and no questions asked.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Yes. The Alaska SeaLife Center is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit under its legal name, Seward Association for the Advancement of Marine Science. Tax ID: 92-0132479. You will receive a year-end giving summary for your records.
How does the Center use my information?
The Alaska SeaLife Center keeps all donor information secure and confidential. Your information is never sold to third parties. You can review the full privacy policy at alaskasealife.org/privacy.
What is the School of Sustainers?
The School of Sustainers is the Alaska SeaLife Center's monthly giving community. Just as salmon thrive in the community of a school, the Center relies on a community of recurring donors to stay strong, season to season. Members receive exclusive impact updates and recognition throughout the year.
How does the Center respond to wildlife emergencies?
The Alaska SeaLife Center operates the only institution in Alaska authorized to rehabilitate live stranded marine mammals. We operate under permits from NOAA and the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, and remain in a ready state year-round to respond to strandings, oil spills, and unusual wildlife events anywhere in Alaska.
Does the Center only serve Seward or all of Alaska?
Both. While the Center is physically located in Seward on the shores of Resurrection Bay, our wildlife response program covers all of Alaska, our research studies populations across the North Pacific, and our education programs reach students in Seward and far beyond through distance learning and outreach.
How is the Alaska SeaLife Center different from a regular aquarium?
The Alaska SeaLife Center is the only facility in Alaska that combines a public aquarium with active marine research, wildlife rehabilitation, and ocean education. Visitors do not just see animals. They see the science happening in real time, because it is the same facility where the research and rescue work actually takes place.
Can I volunteer or get involved beyond donating?
Yes! The Center welcomes volunteers, and there are also internship, externship, and employment opportunities for those who want a deeper connection to the mission. Visit alaskasealife.org/volunteer to learn more about how to get involved in person.
How do I know the Center is financially responsible?
The Alaska SeaLife Center holds GuideStar Platinum status for 2025, the highest level of nonprofit transparency. Full financial statements and annual reports are publicly available at alaskasealife.org/annual_and_financial_reports. The Center is also fully accredited by the Association of Zoos and Aquariums.
